# Xcellent1 Lawn Care — Standard Operating Procedures

**Version 1.0 — 2026-04-01**  
**Service Area:** LaPlace, Norco, Destrehan, Luling, and surrounding St. John the Baptist / St. Charles Parish areas  
**Purpose:** These SOPs define how every job is executed at Xcellent1, whether by a core crew member or a 1099 gig worker. Consistent execution is the product. Deviating from these procedures is grounds for removal from the platform.

---

## Part 1 — Client & Sales

### SOP-01: Client Consultation

Every new client consultation follows this sequence:

1. **Pre-visit research:** Review property via Google Maps satellite view. Note property size, obstacles (trees, beds, fencing), access points.
2. **On-site walkthrough:** Walk the full property with the client. Document scope with photos.
3. **Scope definition:** Confirm exactly what is included (mowing, edging, trimming, blowing, haul-off) before leaving.
4. **Quote delivery:** Send written quote within 24 hours via the app or email. No verbal-only quotes.
5. **Follow-up:** If no response in 48 hours, send one follow-up message. Log outcome in CRM.

**Standard:** Client knows exactly what to expect before first service begins.

---

### SOP-02: Estimating & Quoting

1. Measure property square footage (use MapMeasure or Google Earth).
2. Apply service rate card:
   - Mowing (residential, up to 5,000 sq ft): base rate
   - Each additional 1,000 sq ft: incremental rate
   - Edging, trimming, blowing: included in standard service
   - Haul-off (clippings, debris): add-on, priced by volume
   - Fertilization, aeration, overseeding: separate rate card
3. Factor in drive time if property is outside primary service zone.
4. Add overhead allocation (fuel, equipment wear) per job.
5. Never quote below minimum margin threshold.

**Standard:** Every quote is consistent, defensible, and profitable.

---

### SOP-03: Job Scheduling & Routing

1. Jobs are scheduled geographically — cluster by zip code to minimize drive time.
2. Client receives confirmation 24-48 hours before service via automated SMS.
3. Route is loaded into crew app before day starts. No paper routing.
4. Weather contingency: if rain is forecast, reschedule same-day and notify client immediately.
5. Gig workers claim available job blocks — system auto-clusters nearby blocks for efficiency.

**Standard:** No wasted drive time. Client always knows when we’re coming.

## Part 2 — Job Execution

### SOP-04: Pre-Job Site Setup

1. Park vehicle legally and safely. Do not block driveways or fire hydrants.
2. Conduct 2-minute site walkthrough before unloading equipment:
   - Locate all obstacles (sprinkler heads, decorative rocks, toys, pet waste)
   - Check for utilities flags (call 811 if excavation is involved)
   - Note any client special instructions from the job card
3. Set up equipment: mower, trimmer, edger, blower staged and ready.
4. Safety check: PPE on (eye protection, hearing protection, boots).

**Standard:** Zero surprises mid-job. Equipment ready before starting.

---

### SOP-05: Equipment Pre-Check (Daily)

Before leaving for the day, every crew member checks:

- [ ] Mower blades sharp (replace or sharpen every 25 hours of use)
- [ ] Mower oil level checked
- [ ] Fuel full on all equipment
- [ ] Trimmer line loaded
- [ ] Edger blade intact
- [ ] Blower functional
- [ ] Safety equipment present (glasses, gloves, ear protection)
- [ ] Any damage from previous day reported to owner via app

**Standard:** Equipment is never the reason a job is delayed or done poorly.

---

### SOP-06: Mowing & Turf Care

**Sequence (always in this order):**
1. Remove debris from lawn (sticks, trash, pet waste) before mowing.
2. Mow in straight overlapping passes. Alternate direction each visit to prevent rut patterns.
3. Mow at correct height for Louisiana warm-season grass:
   - St. Augustine: 3.5–4 inches
   - Bermuda: 1.5–2 inches
   - Zoysia: 1–2 inches
4. Trim all edges (fence lines, beds, structures) after mowing.
5. Edge all hard surfaces (sidewalks, driveways, curbs) with edger — not trimmer.
6. Blow all clippings off hard surfaces. Never leave clippings on driveway, sidewalk, or street.
7. Final visual pass: confirm no missed areas, no scalping, clean edges.

**Standard:** Every lawn looks identical regardless of which crew member or gig worker performed the service.

---

### SOP-07: Debris & Cleanup

1. All clippings, trimmings, and debris removed from hard surfaces.
2. If haul-off is included: bag all debris and load into truck. Nothing left on property.
3. If haul-off is NOT included: debris blown to designated area on property per client instruction.
4. Equipment wiped down on-site before loading.
5. Final walkthrough of entire property.
6. Take completion photo (required to close job in app).

**Standard:** Property looks better when we leave than when we arrived.

---

### SOP-08: Quality Inspection & Job Close

1. Before marking job complete in the app:
   - [ ] All areas mowed
   - [ ] All edges trimmed
   - [ ] All hard surfaces blown clean
   - [ ] Debris handled per scope
   - [ ] No equipment damage to property
   - [ ] Completion photo uploaded
2. Mark job complete in app.
3. Client receives automated completion notification + photo.
4. If client is on-site: offer a brief walkthrough.

**Standard:** Job is not complete until the app says it’s complete. No exceptions.

## Part 3 — Financial & Administrative

### SOP-09: Invoice Processing

1. Invoice auto-generates when job is marked complete in app.
2. Invoice delivered to client via email + SMS within 5 minutes of job close.
3. Payment terms: due on receipt for residential. Net-15 for commercial.
4. Accepted payment: card, ACH, Zelle (owner approval required for Zelle).
5. If unpaid after 7 days: automated reminder sent.
6. If unpaid after 14 days: owner alerted. Manual follow-up required.

**Standard:** Cash flow never lags more than 14 days behind completed work.

---

### SOP-10: Accounts Receivable

1. Owner reviews aging report every Monday.
2. Any account >21 days past due: escalate to owner for direct outreach.
3. Repeat late payers: require card-on-file before scheduling future services.
4. Commercial accounts: establish credit limit and deposit requirement before first job.

## Part 4 — Crew & Worker Management

### SOP-11: New Worker Onboarding (Core Crew & Gig Workers)

**Step 1 — Application:**
- Submit application via app
- Provide: name, contact, equipment owned, availability, service areas

**Step 2 — Qualification Gate:**
- Background check (required, FCRA-compliant)
- Equipment verification (mower, trimmer, edger, blower — all operational)
- SOP quiz: must score 80% or higher on questions from this document
- Insurance verification (for gig workers operating their own equipment)

**Step 3 — Onboarding:**
- Review full SOP document (this file)
- Complete app walkthrough (how to claim blocks, upload photos, clock in/out)
- Shadow one job with experienced crew member before solo jobs (core crew only)
- Gig workers: first 3 jobs rated by owner before full platform access

**Standard:** Any qualified person who passes the gate can execute an Xcellent1 job to standard.

---

### SOP-12: Performance Standards

Performance is tracked automatically in the app and displayed to each worker.

| Rating | Threshold | Consequence |
|---|---|---|
| 🟢 Green | ≥95% on-time, ≥95% quality score | Priority access to premium blocks |
| 🟡 Yellow | 80-94% on-time or quality | Warning + coaching message |
| 🔴 Red | <80% on-time or quality | Removed from block queue pending review |

- Quality score is derived from: completion photos reviewed, client rating (1-5), zero property damage reports
- On-time score: job started within 15-minute window of scheduled time
- Owner reviews red-status workers before any reinstatement

---

### SOP-13: Safety Compliance

1. Daily safety check before departing for jobs (see SOP-05).
2. PPE required on every job: safety glasses, hearing protection, closed-toe boots.
3. Never operate equipment in active lightning or flooding conditions.
4. Incident reporting: any equipment damage, property damage, or injury reported in app immediately. Owner notified automatically.
5. All gig workers operating in Louisiana are responsible for their own general liability insurance. Xcellent1 carries platform insurance.

### SOP-15: Seasonal Planning (Louisiana Climate)

Louisiana has a distinct growing season calendar that differs from national lawn care standards:

| Month | Primary Services | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Jan–Feb | Dormant cleanup, leaf removal | Minimal mowing |
| Mar–Apr | Spring startup, pre-emergent, fertilization | Busy season begins |
| May–Sep | Weekly mowing, pest control, irrigation | Peak season — surge pricing applies |
| Oct–Nov | Aeration, overseeding, fall cleanup | Transition season |
| Dec | Leaf cleanup, equipment winterization | Slower but steady |

- Pre-emergent herbicide application: February-March (before crabgrass germination)
- St. Augustine fertilization schedule: April, June, September
- Surge pricing activates during May-September peak — gig block prices increase automatically

## Part 6 — Gig Platform Rules (1099 Workers)

These rules apply specifically to gig workers operating on the Xcellent1 platform. Core crew members follow all SOPs above plus their employment agreement.

1. **You are an independent contractor.** Xcellent1 does not control your schedule, only the job standards.
2. **Claim only blocks you can complete on time.** Abandoning a claimed block is a performance violation.
3. **Your equipment is your responsibility.** Equipment failure does not excuse incomplete jobs.
4. **Client information is confidential.** Do not contact clients outside the app for any reason.
5. **Soliciting clients directly is prohibited.** Any worker found soliciting Xcellent1 clients for personal business is permanently removed from the platform.
6. **You are responsible for your own taxes.** Xcellent1 issues 1099-NEC for earnings over $600/year.
7. **Platform terms of service govern all disputes.** See Terms & Conditions (to be added before platform launch).
