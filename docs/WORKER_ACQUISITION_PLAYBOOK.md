# Xcellent1 Worker Acquisition Playbook

## Goal
Hire and retain 2 more field workers without turning the business into a heavy HR machine.

## Current reality
- Current team: Owner + 3 field workers
- Target: 5 total workers so the business can absorb growth and absences
- Revenue constraint: current monthly revenue is too small to absorb two full-time hires at market wage without a mix of temp/gig coverage
- Pain: hiring is reactive; there is no consistent acquisition funnel
- Existing assets:
  - careers page and application dashboard
  - worker application status tracking
  - SOPs for job execution
  - gig-worker model for surge capacity
  - market research memo: `docs/WORKER_MARKET_RESEARCH.md`

## ERC operating frame
- **Empathize:** the owner needs fast coverage without heavy HR overhead.
- **Realize:** the app already has applications, status tracking, dashboards, and a temp-labor fallback lane.
- **Conceptualize:** the work is a daily admin loop, not a giant org chart.

Use this playbook together with `docs/OWNER_ADMIN_PACK.md` and
`docs/WORKER_MARKET_RESEARCH.md`.




## Management model
Use three parallel lanes:

1. **Core crew lane**
   - Best for dependable, recurring field workers
   - Focus on local applicants who want a steady weekly schedule
   - Use the careers page, referrals, local community posts, and direct outreach

2. **Temp-service lane**
   - Best for immediate coverage when the crew is short-handed
   - Use staffing / temp agencies for short-term labor, not long-term dependence
   - Good local options to start with:
     - Labor Finders — Metairie (general labor, temp-to-hire)
     - AMMON Staffing — Metairie / New Orleans (temporary, PRN, contract labor)
     - PeopleReady — New Orleans area (quick-start general labor)
   - Treat temp workers like a bridge: fast fill, fast evaluation, keep score

3. **Gig-worker lane**
   - Best for surge capacity and seasonal overflow
   - Workers claim job blocks, pass a qualification gate, and get paid per job
   - Requires SOP quiz, equipment verification, and background check gate

## Acquisition funnel

### 1. Attract
- Careers page headline: "Join the Xcellent1 Crew"
- Show pay range, job type, and growth path up front
- Promote:
  - outdoor work
  - predictable pay
  - fast callback
  - crew-lead path
  - part-time/seasonal flexibility

### 2. Capture
- All applicants flow into `applications`
- Tag every applicant source:
  - careers page
  - referral
  - gig-worker invite
  - community post
  - direct outreach

### 3. Screen
- Owner or manager reviews new applications daily
- Reject quickly if:
  - no transportation
  - no availability
  - no basic reliability
- Advance fast if they can start soon and show up consistently

### 4. Interview
- Keep it short:
  - work history
  - availability
  - transportation
  - comfort with outdoor labor
  - willingness to follow SOPs
- Aim to schedule within 48 hours of application

- Give a clear starting role:
  - Field Worker
  - Seasonal Helper
  - Crew Lead candidate
  - Temp-service worker (short-term)
  - 1099 gig worker

### 6. Onboard
- First day checklist:
  - identity verification
  - route familiarity
  - SOP walkthrough
  - photo upload test
  - crew contact list
- For gig workers:
  - background check gate
  - equipment verification
  - SOP quiz
  - first 3 jobs reviewed

### 7. Retain
- Give every new worker:
  - predictable schedule
  - fast communication
  - clear job steps
  - performance feedback
  - referral bonus path

## Operating rules
- Response SLA: every application gets a reply within 24 hours
- Interview target: within 48 hours
- Offer target: same day as interview if qualified
- First job target: within 7 days of application
- Do not leave candidates hanging; slow follow-up kills applicant flow

## Weekly management cadence

### Monday
- Review new applications
- Refresh careers post / social post
- Check open shifts and upcoming labor gaps

### Wednesday
- Conduct interviews
- Send offers
- Confirm next-day start dates

### Friday
- Review worker performance
- Ask for referrals
- Identify who can move into crew lead

## Metrics to manage
- Applications this week
- Interview rate
- Offer rate
- Hire rate
- Time to first job
- First-30-day retention
- Referral hires


## Admin handoff
- Use `docs/OWNER_ADMIN_PACK.md` as the working checklist.
- Treat the careers page, applications dashboard, and temp-service lane as one hiring system.
- Keep the owner updated on headcount, pay range, and coverage gaps before any offer goes out.

## Immediate next actions
1. Make the careers page a real funnel, not just a static page
2. Route every application into a visible dashboard with source tags
3. Add referral bonus messaging
4. Separate core crew hires from gig workers in the data model and UI
5. Track hiring speed as tightly as job completion speed

## What not to do
- Do not overbuild HR software before the funnel exists
- Do not wait on perfect branding before recruiting
- Do not mix applicant tracking with client leads
- Do not rely on one person remembering follow-ups
