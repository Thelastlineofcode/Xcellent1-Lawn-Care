# Legal, Compliance, and Licensing Guide – Xcellent 1 Lawn Care (Louisiana, 2025)

## State of Louisiana – Lawn/Landscape Business Requirements

### Statewide Requirements
- **Legal business structure:** Register as LLC, S-corp, or Sole Proprietor with Louisiana Secretary of State
- **Louisiana Department of Revenue:** State tax registration required
- **Parish/Local License:** St. John the Baptist Parish (Laplace) business/occupational license – annual renewal

### Insurance
- **General Liability Insurance:** REQUIRED ($500K–$1M typical minimum)
- **Workers' Compensation:** REQUIRED if you have any employees (not just contractors)
- **Commercial Auto Insurance:** REQUIRED for all business-owned vehicles
- **Bonding:** Some contracts, especially government/commercial, require surety bonds

### Licensing – By Service Category
- **Landscape Horticulturist License:** Required if offering landscaping, design, large plantings
    - Must pass state exam, maintain continuing ed
    - License number and “Landscape Horticulturist” must appear on all plans
- **Landscape Irrigation Contractor:** Required for irrigation installation/service
    - Written contracts and proof of liability insurance required
    - Must have a Water Supply Protection Endorsement
- **Pesticide/Herbicide Applications:**
    - Require a Commercial Pesticide Applicator License from Louisiana Dept. of Ag & Forestry
    - Specific license classes for turf, ornamentals, etc.
    - Bonding and continuing education are mandatory
- **Tree/Arborist:** Separate Arborist license required for tree surgery, pruning, or removal
- **Projects >$50,000:** Require a contractor’s license from Louisiana State Licensing Board for Contractors

### Compliance – Ongoing
- Maintain all licenses and insurance (renew annually/check expiration)
- Contracts for irrigation must be written and have clear scope/pricing
- All paid employees must be on payroll, covered by workers comp, and taxes withheld
- Sales tax must be collected/remitted if selling physical goods
- Bonding and certificates must be available for inspection on job sites upon request
- Maintain records of pesticide applications for at least 3 years

### Penalties & Risks
- Unlicensed work results in fines ($500–$5,000+), project stoppage, personal liability
- Insurance lapse can result in personal liability for injuries/damages
- Payroll violations (paying employees as contractors to avoid taxes) can trigger audits and heavy penalties
- Non-compliance can void eligibility for commercial/HOA bids and Google Local Services Ads

### Resources & Contacts
- LA Dept. of Agriculture & Forestry Horticulture Commission
- St. John the Baptist Parish License Office
- LA Secretary of State – Business Services

## Recommendation
- Confirm that the business has proper parish, state, and service-specific licenses
- Ensure all policies (liability, workers’ comp, vehicle) are in force and up to date
- Do not offer or advertise services that require licenses/insurance without active status
- Review compliance at every year-end and before quoting large contracts
