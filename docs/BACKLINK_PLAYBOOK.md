# Backlink Acquisition Playbook — Xcellent1 Lawn Care

**Domain:** xcellent1lawncare.com  
**Location:** LaPlace, Louisiana (St. John the Baptist Parish / River Parishes)  
**Current state:** 0 external backlinks, DA 0, GSC 15 clicks / 546 impressions / avg position 7.5  
**Last updated:** 2026-06-10

---

## Strategy overview

Zero backlinks means every link is high-leverage. The order matters:

1. **Tier 1** (this week) — Claim every free directory profile. These are table-stakes for local SEO. Most take <10 minutes each.
2. **Tier 2** (weeks 2–4) — Build partnerships. One link from a real estate agent's site, a property manager, or a local hardware store's "contractor" page is worth 50 directory links.
3. **Tier 3** (weeks 3–6) — Create content assets that Louisiana sites naturally want to reference. These earn links without asking.
4. **Tier 4** (ongoing) — Local press, association memberships, and community sponsorship.

Each tactic lists effort (low/medium/high), link type (follow/noindex/nofollow), and the exact action. **Nofollow links still help local rankings** — Google's local pack algorithm uses them as citation signals.

## Tier 2 — Local partnerships (1 link each, medium effort)

Each partnership produces at least one dofollow link from a locally relevant domain. Pitch a clear value exchange — not "link to my site" but "I'll maintain your model home's lawn for free in exchange for a vendor page mention."

### Real estate agents (highest ROI)

**Target type:** Individual agents and brokerages that serve the River Parishes market.  
**Why:** Every listing needs curb appeal. A realtor who can say "I work with Xcellent1 — they handle all my pre-listing prep" has a concrete value prop for sellers.

**Named targets:**

| Agent/Brokerage | Area | Approach |
|-----------------|------|----------|
| **Latter & Blum — LaPlace** 119 Belle Terre Blvd | LaPlace | Visit in person. Offer free pre-listing lawn prep estimate for any property they list. Ask for: vendor referral page link, word-of-mouth referrals. |
| **RE/MAX River Parishes** | Reserve / Garyville | Same pitch. Offer to do first property as a demo. Ask for link from their "Preferred Vendors" or "Local Partners" page. |
| **Keller Williams Realty Red Stick** | Gonzales / Prairieville | Pitched as: "You serve Ascension + St. John — I service both. Let me make every lawn you list look premium." |
| **Crescent City Living Realty** | LaPlace | Smaller local brokerage — more likely to hand-edit their site to add a partners page. |
| **Pelican State Realty** | Metairie / River Parishes | Covers Metairie + parts of River Parishes. Agent-to-agent referrals can extend reach. |

**What to offer:** Free one-time lawn prep (mow, edge, weed beds, fresh mulch) for every listing they take. Value: ~$150–250 cost to you. At 1–2 listings/month that's under $500/mo for a steady stream of curated photos of your work on staged homes.

**What to ask for:**
- Listed on their website's "Preferred Vendors" page (link to xcellent1lawncare.com)
- Named as the lawn care provider in their new-listing announcement emails/newsletters
- A referral script: "Xcellent1 handles lawn prep for all my listings — mention I sent you and get your first mow free."

**Script for the ask:**

> _Hi [Agent Name], I run Xcellent1 Lawn Care here in LaPlace. I see you list homes in the River Parishes. I'd like to offer free pre-listing lawn prep — mow, edge, weed, and mulch the beds — for every home you list. It means the photos look better, open houses have curb appeal, and your seller loves how much effort you put in. In exchange, I'd ask you to list me on your website as a preferred vendor, and refer buyers/sellers my way when they need ongoing lawn care. Can I buy you coffee at Coffee Rani on Airline and talk through it?_

## Tier 3 — Content assets that earn links (3-4 weeks to produce)

Two blog posts + one resource page that other Louisiana sites will naturally link to.

### Asset 1: "Best Grass Types for South Louisiana Humidity" (linkable resource)

**Format:** Detailed guide with comparison table, photos, and maintenance schedule for each grass type.  
**Target audience:** Louisiana homeowners who type "what grass grows best in Louisiana" or "best grass for Laplace LA"  
**Why it earns links:**

- LSU AgCenter websites, gardening blogs, and local news would reference it
- Real estate/relocation guides (e.g., "Moving to Louisiana? Here's what you need to know about lawns")
- Local lawn forums and Nextdoor posts
- Baton Rouge Sod, local garden centers, and extension offices might link to the "Maintenance Calendar" section

**Structure:**
1. Intro: South Louisiana's climate (Zone 9a, high humidity, heavy rain, heat)
2. Comparison table: St. Augustine vs Bermuda vs Centipede vs Zoysia (shade tolerance, water needs, maintenance, best use)
3. Maintenance calendar by month: what to do Jan–Dec
4. Common problems: chinch bugs, brown patch, take-all root rot (very Louisiana-specific)
5. Pro tips section: mowing height, watering schedule, fertilizing schedule
6. CTA: "Need help maintaining your lawn? Xcellent1 serves the River Parishes — get a free quote."

**Outreach targets to notify when published:**
- LSU AgCenter turfgrass program (email turf@agcenter.lsu.edu — they may link to a well-written guide)
- Louisiana Garden Guy (Dan Gill, retired LSU AgCenter — email his current outlet)
- Baton Rouge Sod (batonrougesod.com — they'd link a guide that mentions sod types)
- Each local garden center / nursery's blog

### Asset 2: "LaPlace Lawn Care Calendar: What to Do Every Month" (seasonal, earned links)

**Format:** Printable PDF + blog post. Month-by-month checklist for Louisiana lawns.  
**Why it earns links:**

- Year-round relevance, updated annually
- HOA newsletters, realtor timing guides, and local Facebook groups share monthly lawn tips
- Local newspapers (L'Observateur front page sidebar) might reference a printable calendar
- Every spring and fall people search "when to fertilize lawn in Louisiana" — Google surfaces this type of content

**Structure:**
1. Short intro about why South Louisiana is different
2. Monthly table: Month | Task | Product (optional) | Notes
3. Key events: when to aerate (April/Sept), when to overseed, when to apply pre-emergent (Feb/March)
4. Printable PDF download (collect email for it)

**Outreach targets:**
- L'Observateur — offer to write a 500-word "seasonal lawn tips for River Parishes homeowners" guest column
- Nextdoor neighborhoods in LaPlace/Reserve/Gramercy
- Local garden clubs (River Parishes Garden Club, St. John Garden Club)

### Asset 3: "River Parishes Lawn & Garden Resource Guide" (local hub page)

**Format:** One page that collects every local resource a Louisiana homeowner needs — sorted by topic. This becomes the linkable hub for the whole local lawn care ecosystem.

**Sections:**
- **Soil testing** — link to LSU AgCenter soil test lab (free/low-cost soil testing)
- **Local sod suppliers** — names and locations (Baton Rouge Sod, Delta Sod, etc.)
- **Recommended fertilizers** — brands that work in South LA soils
- **Pest control calendar** — chinch bugs, armyworms, mole crickets, fire ants
- **Irrigation tips** — watering restrictions for St. John Parish
- **Local equipment repair** — small engine shops in LaPlace/Reserve
- **Louisiana-friendly plant list** — shrubs, trees, flowers that survive the heat/humidity

**Why it earns links:**

- LSU AgCenter, extension offices, nurseries, and local blogs will link to a page that aggregates their tools
- Real estate agents link to it in "moving to LaPlace" guides
- Becomes the #1 organic hit for "lawn care resources River Parishes"

**Outreach targets for this asset specifically:**
- LSU AgCenter St. John Parish extension agent
- St. John Parish government website (parish.stjohn.la.us)
- River Region Chamber of Commerce (offer it as a member resource)
- L'Observateur business section (they may link it as a community resource)
