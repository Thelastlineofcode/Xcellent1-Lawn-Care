# Louisiana Vendor Registration Guide

## What Is LA Vendor Registration?

Louisiana Vendor Registration is the mandatory process for any business seeking to contract with the State of Louisiana or its political subdivisions (parishes, school boards, municipalities, special districts). Registration is done through the **LaGov Vendor Portal** (Louisiana's centralized procurement system):

- **Portal**: [LaGov ERP Vendor Portal](https://lagoverpvendorportal.doa.la.gov)
- **Agency**: Louisiana Division of Administration, Office of State Procurement
- **Requirement**: Any vendor doing business with a Louisiana public entity must maintain an active registration

Registration covers all state agencies, parish governments, school districts, and local municipalities that use the LaGov procurement system.

## Step-by-Step Registration

### Step 1: Create a LaGov Vendor Account

1. Go to [https://lagoverpvendorportal.doa.la.gov](https://lagoverpvendorportal.doa.la.gov)
2. Click **"New Vendor Registration"** or **"Create Account"**
3. Provide:
   - Business legal name (exact match to EIN filing)
   - DBA ("Xcellent1 Lawn Care" if registered as DBA)
   - Taxpayer identification (EIN or SSN)
   - Business address, phone, email
4. Set up login credentials and security questions

### Step 2: Complete the Business Profile

- **Business Type**: Lawn Care / Grounds Maintenance
- **Primary NAICS Code**: **561730** — Landscaping Services
- **Secondary NAICS Codes** (add as applicable):
  - 561790 — Other Services to Buildings and Dwellings
  - 238990 — All Other Specialty Trade Contractors
  - 115310 — Support Activities for Forestry (vegetation management)

### Step 3: Upload Required Documents

| Document | Details |
|---|---|
| **EIN Confirmation Letter** | IRS-issued CP 575 or 147C |
| **Business License** | Louisiana state or local parish license |
| **Insurance Certificate** | General Liability, minimum $1,000,000 per occurrence |
| **Workers' Comp Certificate** | Required for any contract over $25k (or if bids reference it) |
| **Contractor's License** | Not always required for landscaping under $50k, but check parish limits |

### Step 4: Certify and Submit

1. Review all information for accuracy
2. Certify under penalty of perjury
3. Submit — registration is typically processed within 1-3 business days
4. Save the confirmation/registration number for all future bids

## DBE Certification — LA Small Business Subcontracting Pool

The **Louisiana Department of Transportation and Development (DOTD)** manages a **Disadvantaged Business Enterprise (DBE)** program for highway and infrastructure contracts.

### What It Is

- Federal certification administered by DOTD through the **LA Unified Certification Program (LAUCP)**
- Preferred status for bidding on DOTD contracts and DOTD-funded local projects
- Opens the door to highway vegetation control, median mowing, and roadside maintenance — contracts often $20k - $100k+

### Why It Matters

- DOTD projects are federally mandated to meet DBE participation goals
- Prime contractors actively seek DBE subcontractors
- Less competition than open bidding

### How to Apply

1. Contact: **LA DOTD DBE Program** — (225) 379-1316 or visit [dotd.la.gov](https://www.dotd.la.gov)
2. Certification requirements mirror federal DBE guidelines
3. Must be at least 51% owned and controlled by a socially and economically disadvantaged individual(s)
4. Prepare: personal net worth statement, business tax returns, management structure documentation
