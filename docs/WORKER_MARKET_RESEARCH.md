## Purpose
Ground the worker acquisition plan in the local market, the labor pool, and the money math.

Use this memo with `docs/OWNER_ADMIN_PACK.md` and

## Local market snapshot

- Median household income (2020–2024 ACS): **$66,630**
- Poverty rate: **19.0%**
- Civilian labor force participation: **60.0%**

- Total employment (2023): **15,023**

Source: U.S. Census Bureau QuickFacts for St. John the Baptist Parish, Louisiana.

## Local wage floor for lawn labor

### Landscaping & groundskeeping wages
- Louisiana median hourly wage (2024): **$15.64/hr**
- New Orleans-Metairie metro median hourly wage (2024): **$15.87/hr**
- New Orleans-Metairie metro annual median wage (2024): **$33,020**
- Louisiana annual median wage (2024): **$32,540**

Source: O*NET Local Wages / BLS OEWS 2024 data.

### What that means
- [Inference] If Xcellent1 wants reliable worker fill, it should assume direct-hire starting pay needs to sit **at or above the local landscaping median**.
- [Inference] For the River Parishes / New Orleans metro, a starting rate below the local median will likely produce weak applicant volume.
- [Inference] Temp-service workers will cost more than the worker wage itself, so use them only when the value of immediate coverage is higher than the markup.

### 3) AMMON Staffing — New Orleans / Metairie

## Business implications

### What the numbers suggest
1. The parish is small enough that **referrals and local community channels matter**.
2. The local wage floor is not high enough to ignore; **worker pay must be competitive**.
3. The business should not rely on one hiring lane.
4. **Temp services are a coverage tool**, not the primary hiring engine.
5. **Gig workers** are a better fit for overflow and block-based demand than for stable week-to-week crew planning.

## Recommended staffing stack

### Core crew
- Use for dependable recurring work.
- Recruit locally.
- Keep the response SLA short.

### Temp services
- Use for:
  - sick days
  - weather reschedules
  - peak weeks
  - last-minute coverage gaps
- Best agencies to try first:
  - Labor Finders Metairie
  - AMMON Staffing New Orleans / Metairie
  - PeopleReady New Orleans

### Gig workers
- Use for:
  - overflow blocks
  - seasonal spikes
  - weekend-only coverage
- Keep a qualification gate and SOP quiz.

## Revenue math
- Current revenue (from reality check): **~$8K/month**
- Target revenue: **~$12K/month**
- [Inference] A full-time worker at the New Orleans metro landscaping median (~$15.87/hr) costs about **$2,750/month** before payroll taxes, tools, and admin.
- [Inference] Two full-time workers at that rate would be about **$5,500/month** before overhead, which is most of the current monthly revenue.
- [Inference] That means the next hires should probably be a mix of:
  - part-time core crew
  - temp-service coverage
  - gig-worker overflow
  until monthly revenue climbs closer to the target.

## Money guidance
- Track **cost per filled shift** for each lane:
  - core crew
  - temp service
  - gig worker
- Track **cost of an unfilled shift** too:
  - missed revenue
  - customer churn
  - owner time spent covering jobs
- [Inference] The right lane is the one with the lowest **total cost of coverage**, not the cheapest hourly rate.

## Operational recommendation
- Keep the careers funnel, but add temp services as a **fallback lane**.
- If the crew is below the minimum coverage threshold, call temp services before letting jobs slip.
- Use temp labor to protect cash flow while core hiring continues.
- Measure whether temp coverage actually buys enough time to recruit the right long-term hire.
