## CONTEXT & CONSTRAINTS

Lacardio is currently licensed for **landscaping/lawn maintenance only** in Louisiana. This means he can legally perform:
- Lawn mowing, edging, trimming
- Leaf/debris cleanup & hauling
- Mulching (installation, not chemically treated)
- Planting ornamentals, sod installation
- Basic landscape bed maintenance
- Flower bed prep, soil amendment (non-pesticide)
- Hardscape cleanup (blowing, raking)

He **cannot** yet perform (training modules to unlock):
- Pesticide/herbicide application → requires Louisiana Department of Agriculture & Forestry (LDAF) Pesticide Applicator License
- Irrigation installation/repair → requires Louisiana State Plumbing Board Irrigation Contractor License
- Tree removal/trimming above ground level (commercial) → requires Louisiana Horticulture Commission Licensed Horticulturist or arborist credential
- Fertilizer application (commercial) → LDAF oversight; some contexts require licensed applicator
- General contracting work → Louisiana State Licensing Board for Contractors (LSLBC)

## SOP-001: PRE-DAY PREPARATION

**Trigger:** Every work morning before leaving base  
**Owner:** Lacardio  
**Time Budget:** 20–30 min  

### Checklist
- [ ] Review job schedule in Xcellent1 app — confirm addresses, service types, notes
- [ ] Load trailer/truck: mower, edger, trimmer, blower, rakes, tarps, fuel cans
- [ ] Check fuel levels — mower, trimmer, blower (fill at base, not on route)
- [ ] Check blade condition — sharp blades cut 30% faster and reduce engine strain
- [ ] Verify PPE loaded: gloves, safety glasses, ear protection, steel-toe boots
- [ ] Check weather forecast — cancel/reschedule if >50% rain probability (wet grass = poor cut quality + safety; River Parishes get afternoon pop-up storms April–October)
- [ ] Confirm client contact info and gate codes are in app
- [ ] Plan route — optimize by geographic cluster (LaPlace → Reserve → Lutcher → Gramercy → Destrehan sequence avoids backtracking on Hwy 61 and River Road)

### ERC Note
The River Parishes are a linear corridor along the Mississippi. Route jobs along Hwy 61 or River Road in one direction — never zigzag. A poor route on this corridor can waste 60–90 min/day in unnecessary driving.

## SOP-002: JOB SITE ARRIVAL

**Trigger:** Every client arrival  
**Time Budget:** 5 min  

### Checklist
- [ ] Park clear of driveways, mailboxes, and fire hydrants
- [ ] Walk the property before unloading — identify obstacles: toys, hoses, dog waste, sprinkler heads, decorative rocks, exposed roots (common in River Parish lots with mature live oaks)

### ERC Note
River Parish lots frequently have mature live oaks with surface roots and low canopy. Identify these before mowing — scalping roots damages trees and exposes you to liability.

## SOP-003: LAWN MOWING SERVICE

**Trigger:** Any mowing job  
**Time Budget:** 30–90 min depending on lot size  

### Execution Sequence
1. **Set mowing height** — River Parish standard: St. Augustine 3–4 inches (dominant grass in South Louisiana), Bermuda 1.5–2 inches, Centipede 1.5–2 inches. Never remove more than 1/3 of blade height in one cut.
2. **Mow pattern** — alternate direction each visit to prevent ruts and train grass upright
3. **Overlap rows** by ~2 inches to prevent missed strips
4. **Discharge direction** — always discharge away from beds, cars, windows, people
5. **Mow perimeter first**, then fill interior in pattern
6. **Bag or mulch** per client preference (note in job record)

### Checklist
- [ ] Blade height set correctly for grass type
- [ ] Discharge chute cleared before starting
- [ ] All obstacles removed or flagged
- [ ] Pattern logged (alternate each visit)
- [ ] Clippings managed per client preference

### ERC Note
St. Augustine dominates River Parish residential. It requires a higher cut (3–4 in) than most other regions — cutting too short stresses the turf and leads to client callbacks. Document grass type in every client profile.

## SOP-004: EDGING SERVICE

**Trigger:** Every mowing visit or standalone edge job  
**Time Budget:** 15–45 min depending on linear footage  

### Execution Sequence
1. Edge along all **concrete borders** first: driveway, sidewalk, curb
2. Edge along **bed borders** second
3. Maintain a consistent angle (90° for clean definition)
4. Remove edging debris with blower, not broom (faster)
5. Inspect edge line from street view before moving on

### Checklist
- [ ] Edger blade inspected (replace when worn — dull edges tear, not cut)
- [ ] All concrete borders done
- [ ] All bed borders done
- [ ] No turf clumps left in beds or on hardscape

### ERC Note
A clean edge is the single biggest visual signal of quality work in tight-knit River Parish neighborhoods where houses are close together and neighbors talk. Contracts renew on the strength of edges.

## SOP-005: WEED-EATING / STRING TRIMMING

**Trigger:** Every mowing visit  
**Time Budget:** 15–30 min  

### Execution Sequence
1. Trim around all **vertical obstacles**: fence posts, tree bases, utility poles, mailboxes
2. Trim around **beds** where mower can't reach
3. Keep trimmer head parallel to ground (tilting scalps turf)
4. Use guard — string breakage causes debris injury
5. Extra care around live oak and cypress bases — root flares are often exposed and delicate in South Louisiana soils

### Checklist
- [ ] Trimmer line loaded and adequate length
- [ ] All tree bases trimmed (no scalping of root flares)
- [ ] Fence line completed
- [ ] No missed patches visible from street view

## SOP-006: BLOWING / CLEANUP

**Trigger:** End of every service visit  
**Time Budget:** 10–20 min  

### Execution Sequence
1. Blow all **hardscape** first: driveways, sidewalks, patios
2. Blow all **clippings back into lawn** or into haul pile
3. Blow **street curb** in front of property (professional finish signal)
4. Bag debris if included in service — check St. John the Baptist Parish solid waste pickup schedule before leaving debris at curb

### Checklist
- [ ] No clippings on driveway, walkways, or street
- [ ] Curb line clean
- [ ] No debris blown into neighbor property
- [ ] Gate closed and secured before leaving

## SOP-007: MULCH INSTALLATION

**Trigger:** Mulch service booking  
**Time Budget:** 1–4 hours depending on cubic yard volume  

### Execution Sequence
1. Calculate cubic yards needed: (L x W x Depth in feet) / 27
2. Edge beds before mulching (mulch seals the edge line)
3. Clear existing mulch if >4 inches deep (old mulch suffocates roots)
4. Install at **2–3 inch depth** — no more (mulch volcanoes kill trees)
5. Keep mulch **2–3 inches away from plant stems and tree trunks**
6. Blow off any mulch on hardscape after installation

### Checklist
- [ ] Beds edged before mulch laid
- [ ] Depth measured (not estimated)
- [ ] No mulch volcanoes around tree bases
- [ ] Mulch type matches client preference or bid spec
- [ ] Delivery receipt/invoice photographed for records

### ERC Note
Mulch jobs are high-margin. Upsell mulch refresh on every mowing visit where beds look faded. Pine straw is popular and widely preferred in South Louisiana — always have it as an option.

## SOP-008: PLANTING / ORNAMENTAL INSTALLATION

**Trigger:** Planting service booking  
**Time Budget:** 1–6 hours  

### Execution Sequence
1. Confirm plant species appropriate for **South Louisiana climate zone (Zone 9a/9b)** — high humidity, clay-heavy soils, flood-prone yards are common in River Parishes
2. Dig hole **2x width, same depth** as root ball
3. Loosen roots before placing in hole
4. Backfill with native soil — amend with compost if client requests and soil drainage is poor (common in low-lying LaPlace/Reserve lots)
5. Water in thoroughly at installation
6. Mulch around base (2–3 inch clearance from stem)
7. Photograph completed install for portfolio/records

### Checklist
- [ ] Plant species confirmed for Zone 9 compatibility
- [ ] Hole dimensions correct
- [ ] Root ball not disturbed excessively
- [ ] Watered in at installation
- [ ] Client instruction sheet left for watering schedule

### ERC Note
Many River Parish lots have drainage challenges — always note standing water areas during arrival walk and factor into plant placement. Poor drainage kills plants and creates callbacks.

## SOP-009: SOD INSTALLATION

**Trigger:** Sod service booking  
**Time Budget:** Half day to full day depending on sq footage  

### Execution Sequence
1. Measure area precisely — order 10% overage
2. Till or loosen existing soil 2–4 inches
3. Grade for drainage (slope away from structure — critical in River Parish flood-risk areas)
4. Lay sod in **brick pattern** (staggered joints)
5. Butt edges tightly — no gaps, no overlap
6. Roll sod after installation (rented roller)
7. Water immediately and thoroughly

### Checklist
- [ ] Soil prepped and graded
- [ ] Sod type matches existing lawn (St. Augustine standard for River Parish residential)
- [ ] Staggered joint pattern
- [ ] Edges cut flush at borders
- [ ] Client informed of 14-day establishment watering schedule

## SOP-010: LEAF & DEBRIS CLEANUP

**Trigger:** Cleanup service booking or seasonal add-on  
**Time Budget:** 1–4 hours  

### Execution Sequence
1. Blow all debris to central pile(s)
2. Tarp and haul — confirm with St. John the Baptist Parish solid waste rules before leaving debris at curb
3. Check under live oaks, azaleas, and in beds — River Parish yards accumulate heavy leaf debris under mature trees
4. Final blow of all hardscape

### Checklist
- [ ] All leaf debris removed from turf
- [ ] Bed interiors cleared
- [ ] Hardscape blown clean
- [ ] Haul receipts photographed if dump fee applicable (tax deductible)

### ERC Note
Live oak leaf drop in South Louisiana happens late winter (Jan–March), not fall. Market a "Late Winter Cleanup" service specifically for the River Parishes — most competitors miss this upsell window.

## SOP-012: END-OF-DAY EQUIPMENT MAINTENANCE

**Trigger:** Every work day  
**Time Budget:** 20–30 min  

### Checklist
- [ ] Clean grass clippings from all equipment (prevents corrosion — South Louisiana humidity accelerates rust)
- [ ] Check air filters (weekly minimum — high humidity and pollen loads clog filters faster in South Louisiana)
- [ ] Check oil levels (weekly minimum — document)
- [ ] Sharpen or swap blades (every 25 operating hours)
- [ ] Coil and store extension cords/fuel lines properly
- [ ] Log fuel used and equipment hours in maintenance log
- [ ] Secure trailer — padlock hitch and door

### ERC Note
South Louisiana humidity is brutal on equipment. Daily cleaning and dry storage is not optional — it's the difference between a 5-year mower and a 2-year mower.

## SOP-013: SCHEDULING & ROUTE OPTIMIZATION

**Owner:** Lacardio + Xcellent1 app  

### Rules
1. **Corridor clustering**: River Parishes run east–west along Hwy 61 and River Road. Book jobs in one directional sweep — LaPlace → Reserve → Edgard → Lutcher → Gramercy → Destrehan or reverse. Never backtrack.
2. **Time buffers**: Allow 15-min buffer between jobs (train crossings on River Road, bridge delays on I-310 and I-10 are common)
3. **Recurring clients first**: Lock recurring mowing accounts on a fixed day-of-week. Consistency = retention in a word-of-mouth market like River Parishes.
4. **Weather cancellations**: Reschedule within 24 hours. Text client same day. Summer afternoon thunderstorms are near-daily June–September — schedule sensitive work in morning slots.
5. **No-shows**: Call after 10 min, text after 20 min. Log in app. Bill if within cancellation window.

## APPENDIX: RIVER PARISHES OPERATING NOTES

- **Mowing Season**: Year-round in South Louisiana. Peak growth April–October. Reduce frequency Dec–Feb but don't stop — winters are mild.
- **Grass Types**: St. Augustine dominates River Parish residential. Centipede is common in older lots. Know the difference — St. Augustine requires 3–4 inch cut height; cutting it short like Bermuda will stress the turf and draw client complaints.
- **Heat & Humidity Protocol**: Heat index regularly exceeds 105°F+ June–September. Take 15-min shaded breaks every hour during heat index >105°F (OSHA heat illness prevention guidance). Hydrate continuously.
- **Hurricane Season** (June–November): Maintain flexible cancellation policy. Pre-storm cleanup and post-storm debris removal are high-demand, premium-priced services. Position for both.
- **Flooding**: Many LaPlace, Reserve, and Norco lots have low elevation and poor drainage. Always note standing water during arrival walk. Post-flood cleanup is a recurring upsell opportunity after significant rain events.
- **Live Oak Debris**: South Louisiana live oaks drop leaves Jan–March (not fall). Market a Late Winter Cleanup add-on proactively.
- **Parish Solid Waste**: St. John the Baptist Parish and St. Charles Parish have specific yard waste collection schedules. Confirm pickup days by address before leaving debris at curb. Avoid fines.
- **Train Crossings**: River Road and Hwy 61 corridor has multiple active rail crossings. Factor 10-min delays into route buffers.
