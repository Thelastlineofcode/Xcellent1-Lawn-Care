Best Grass Types for LaPlace, Louisiana

Choosing the right grass type is essential for a lush and healthy lawn in LaPlace and the surrounding River Parishes. Here are the top grass varieties suited for this region:

St. Augustine Grass

Known for its shade tolerance and vibrant green color, St. Augustine grass thrives in the humid climate of Louisiana. It requires moderate maintenance and is resistant to pests common in the River Parishes.

Bermuda Grass

Bermuda grass is highly durable and thrives well in full sun areas. It's drought-resistant and perfect for high-traffic lawns in LaPlace.

Zoysia Grass

Zoysia grass offers a dense turf with great wear tolerance. It does well in Louisiana's soil and requires less watering compared to other grasses.

Centipede Grass

Centipede grass is a low-maintenance, affordable option that thrives in acidic soils common in the River Parishes. It requires less fertilization and mowing.

Best Grass Types for LaPlace, Louisiana

November 2025

Choosing the right grass type for your LaPlace lawn is crucial for maintaining a healthy, beautiful yard in Louisiana's humid subtropical climate. The River Parishes region experiences hot, humid summers and mild winters, making certain grass varieties more suitable than others.

Top Grass Types for LaPlace

üåø St. Augustine Grass

Best for: Homeowners who want a lush, carpet-like lawn with minimal maintenance.

St. Augustine is the most popular grass in LaPlace and throughout Louisiana. It thrives in our humid climate and creates a thick, beautiful turf that handles shade better than most warm-season grasses. Popular varieties include Palmetto, Raleigh, and Floratam.

  • Pros: Excellent shade tolerance, fast-growing, beautiful emerald color
  • Cons: Requires regular watering, susceptible to chinch bugs
  • Maintenance: Mow at 3-4 inches, water 1-2 times weekly

üåæ Bermuda Grass

Best for: Full sun areas, high-traffic lawns, and sports fields.

Bermuda grass is incredibly durable and handles heavy foot traffic exceptionally well. It's the go-to choice for sunny yards and athletic fields in the River Parishes. Common varieties include Tifway 419 and Celebration.

  • Pros: Extremely drought-tolerant, wear-resistant, quick recovery
  • Cons: Goes dormant in winter, poor shade tolerance, aggressive growth
  • Maintenance: Mow at 1-2 inches, needs full sun (8+ hours daily)

üçÉ Zoysia Grass

Best for: Low-maintenance lawns with moderate shade.

Zoysia offers a middle ground between St. Augustine and Bermuda. It's becoming increasingly popular in LaPlace due to its low maintenance requirements and tolerance for both sun and moderate shade. Empire and Emerald are common varieties.

  • Pros: Dense turf resists weeds, moderate shade tolerance, low water needs
  • Cons: Slow to establish, brown in winter, expensive initial installation
  • Maintenance: Mow at 1.5-2.5 inches, water less frequently than St. Augustine

üå± Centipede Grass

Best for: Budget-conscious homeowners wanting low maintenance.

Centipede is known as the "lazy man's grass" because it requires less mowing, fertilizer, and water than other varieties. While not as common in LaPlace as St. Augustine, it's a great choice for low-maintenance properties.

  • Pros: Minimal fertilizer needs, slow growth means less mowing, good for acidic soil
  • Cons: Slow to recover from damage, iron deficiency common, poor cold tolerance
  • Maintenance: Mow at 1.5-2 inches, fertilize sparingly

For most LaPlace homeowners, we recommend St. Augustine grass due to its excellent performance in our climate and beautiful appearance. If you have a sunny yard with heavy foot traffic, Bermuda grass might be better. For low-maintenance properties, consider Centipede or Zoysia.
